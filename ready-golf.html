Ready Golf Guidelines

Ready Golf helps everyone keep pace and enjoy the round. Use common sense, be safe, and keep moving.

In General

Keep pace with the group ahead. Be ready to hit when it’s your turn.

On the Tee

  • On our first hole (#1), don’t tee off until the group in front of you is on the green.
  • Shorter distance hitters should hit first; if not, the first player who is ready should hit.
  • Tee off as soon as the group ahead is clear.
  • Carry an extra golf ball in case you need to hit a provisional ball.

On the Fairway

  • Take several clubs to your ball and hit without delay.
  • Hit when ready; do not wait for honors.
  • Watch your ball.
  • Drop off your cart partner then drive to your own ball.
  • Limit practice swings.
  • Limit search for lost balls to 3 minutes.
  • Pick up when you reach net double bogey.

On the Green

  • Park your cart/clubs behind the green in the direction of the next tee box.
  • Study your putt while others are putting out.
  • Continue putting until you hole out.
  • First one in, gets the pin; if your group is lagging, the first two players who finish putting should head to the next tee box.
  • Mark scores on the next tee box.
